Breakfest:

The Breakfast Trio

The Breakfast Trio is a classic Egyptian morning delight that combines three essential components: crispy taameya, fluffy eggs, and warm baladi bread. This hearty meal is perfect for starting your day with traditional flavors.

Kababgy Fava Delight

A hearty bowl of fava bean stew, spiced with cumin and garlic, served with warm pita bread and a side of tangy pickles. Perfectly balanced flavors that provide a nutritious start to your day.

Shakshuka

Traditional shakshuka with poached eggs nestled in a rich tomato sauce, seasoned with bell peppers and topped with fresh herbs. Served with crusty bread for dipping, this dish combines warmth and zest for a delightful breakfast experience.

Famous Egyptian Food

Koshari

A delicious mix of rice, pasta, lentils, and tomato sauce.

Ful Medames

A staple dish made from fava beans, typically eaten for breakfast.

Molokhia

A green soup made from finely chopped jute leaves, served with rice or bread.

dessert

Baasbousa

Basbousa is a semolina-based cake that is sweetened with sugar syrup and often flavored with almonds or coconut. At Asr El Kababgy, this dessert is typically served in a diamond shape, showcasing its beautiful golden crust. The cake is moist and fluffy, with a syrup soaked into it, giving each bite a rich sweetness that pairs perfectly with a cup of Arabic coffee.

Umm Ali

This is a classic Egyptian dessert, often referred to as the Egyptian bread pudding. Umm Ali is made from layers of flaky pastry, mixed with a creamy blend of milk, sugar, and nuts such as pistachios and almonds. It is baked until golden and served warm, allowing the flavors to meld beautifully. Topped with raisins and a sprinkle of cinnamon, this dessert is both comforting and indulgent.

Konafa

A beloved Middle Eastern dessert, Konafa consists of thin noodle-like pastry that is layered with a mixture of nuts, sweet cheese, or cream. At Asr El Kababgy, their Konafa is usually served warm, drenched in a fragrant sugar syrup infused with rose or orange blossom water. The crispy exterior contrasts delightfully with the soft, sweet filling, making it a favorite among both locals and visitors.
